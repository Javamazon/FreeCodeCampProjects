Anita Borg

Black and white photograph of Anita Borg (January 17, 1949 - April 6, 2003)

Anita Borg was an American computer scientist who advocated for more women to become involved in the computer science and other technology fields. She founded the Institute for Women and Technology as well as the Grace Hopper Celebration of Women in Computing. Borg taught herself programming while working at a small insurance company. She got her first job in 1969. In 1981 she was awarded a PhD. in Computer Science by New York University for her research in investigating the synchronization efficiency of operating systems.

Throughout her life, Anita Borg advocated for a greater representation of women in technology. Her goal was to have a 50% representation level for women in computing by 2020.
